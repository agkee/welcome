A Global Journey

My childhood was defined by constant movement between cultures—from Korea to Russia, US, and Canada, back to US, Japan, UK, and finally back to US. This highly international and mobile upbringing instilled in me a profound adaptability.

Key Insight: Where many feel stress in the face of uncertainty, I feel excitement for "what's next to come." My early life naturally trained me to navigate the unknown.

Physical distance can create profound emotional closeness when it forces intentionality. True, lasting connection is an active practice, not a passive state.

"Where many feel stress in the face of uncertainty, I feel excitement for what's next to come. Adaptability isn't just a skill - it's my superpower."

Future Vision

My ideal week in 5-10 years is defined by being in a room with amazing, inspirational people and engaging in deep conversations about the future of technology, products, and the world. I see myself not as a student or observer, but as a peer and core contributor.

This vision is the ultimate manifestation of my identity as a "Bridge," placing me at the intersection of innovation and communication, where I can help shape the future and ensure it is widely understood and accessible.

Personal Dream Experiences

Beyond professional aspirations, I'm driven by a desire for unique and profound experiences. Whether it's exploring the pristine wilderness of Antarctica or one day setting foot on the Moon, I believe that extraordinary experiences fuel extraordinary thinking and innovation.

Connection Philosophy

🌱 Inner Circle: Deep Roots

My long-standing, deep friendships are my foundation for introspection and self-discovery. These are relationships where I engage in thought-provoking conversations to explore and shape my own core values and ideas, and to understand those of others.

"Quality over quantity - meaningful connections that last decades."

🌐 Outer Network: Wide Branches

I actively build a broader network of friends and acquaintances because I believe that opportunity comes from people. I've adapted the Korean saying about having "three chances in life," believing that by being a good person and cultivating a strong network, I can multiply those chances to 30 or 300.

"Every connection is a potential doorway to unexpected opportunities."

What Drives Me

⚡ Energizers

Projects with autonomy and tangible impact

I feel most energized and "alive" when working on projects that offer complete creative freedom and where I can see the direct, positive effect on people in real-time. My side project leading a program for SNU students in Silicon Valley perfectly exemplifies this - I owned the entire vision and could witness the immediate positive impact on students' lives.

Pure Flow State: Autonomy + Visible Impact = Peak Performance

😴 Demotivators

Massive but abstract impact with constraints

I feel demotivated in environments where the impact is intellectually massive but emotionally abstract, and my creative freedom is constrained. In corporate roles, while I understand my work affects millions, the lack of direct, visible connection to those users makes the impact feel impersonal. Corporate restrictions on new ideas further dampen my motivation.

Energy Drain: Abstract Impact + Limited Autonomy = Frustration
